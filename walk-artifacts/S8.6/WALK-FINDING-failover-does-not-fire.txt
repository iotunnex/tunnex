EPIC-8 WALK FINDING (S8.6, MERGE-GATING) — automatic failover does NOT fire on primary death
The headline S8.6 feature (hub HA) does not fail over on live iron. Wire-proven.

SETUP: aws-site hub set v-N = {aws-gw PRIMARY (pin#1), aws-gw-2 STANDBY (pin#2)}, azure-gw leaf. Standby
provably WARM before the kill (aws-gw-2 <-> azure-gw site-link handshaking, 180 B..1.4 KB, keepalive 25s).
Continuity ping Mac -> 172.31.17.64 (via the aws hub) replying ttl-63.

KILL: `docker stop tunnex-node` + `ip link del wg0` on aws-gw (real data-plane outage, no instance-stop so the
public IP survives for fail-back). T0 noted.

RESULT (~5 min post-kill):
- ping: gapped at seq 196, NEVER recovered.
- Sites hub-set: STUCK at hub set v4 — aws-gw still PRIMARY (badge "stale", handshake 4m), aws-gw-2 STANDBY
  (badge "warm", handshake 56s). NO promotion.
- aws-site card: aws-gw HUB "offline", aws-gw-2 "online". azure-site: azure-gw "site link down" (lost its
  dead-primary site-link, never repointed to the standby).
- AUDIT LOG: last hub_set.promotion is 7m ago (BEFORE the kill). ZERO promotion events after the kill.
  (The pre-kill promotions all had new_primary == old_primary == aws-gw — the controller re-electing the same
   node — but those were setup-phase; the point is nothing fired post-kill.)

MECHANISM (code): RunFailoverTick every FailoverTickInterval=30s -> failoverOrg. failoverStaleWindow=90s.
failoverOrg computes freshness[id] = node_peer_status LastHandshakeAt within 90s (ListNodePeerStatusForOrg +
latestByPubKey), Step() demotes a member with N consecutive stale ticks, deriveActive(configured, demoted)
moves demoted to the back. deriveActive is CORRECT. The break: the controller's freshness read shows aws-gw
FRESH (so Step never demotes it) EVEN THOUGH the hub-set VIEW shows it stale (handshake 4m). A source
disagreement between the failover freshness (node_peer_status/latestByPubKey) and the view — OR the dead
primary's peer-status LastHandshakeAt is not aging as the controller expects (who reports a dead hub's
handshake once its peers' own links die?). Net: primary_stale is never detected by the CONTROLLER post-kill,
so no demotion, no promotion, no recompile, no device re-point, no recovery.

IMPACT: HA's entire promise — automatic promotion of the warm standby when the primary dies — does not work.
A killed primary strands all its traffic indefinitely; the warm standby sits idle. This is the core of S8.6.

DISPOSITION: HELD for founder — MERGE-GATING candidate for S8.6. Needs a DECISION-FIRST fix (its own commit-one
+ reds), NOT a walk-window patch: the freshness-source-of-truth for hub-member liveness (node_peer_status vs the
view), who keeps a dead hub's LastHandshakeAt aging, and a red that KILLS a primary in a fake clock and asserts
the standby is promoted within N ticks. Deck B's failover proof is BLOCKED on this. The kill did its job: it
proved automatic failover does not happen.
